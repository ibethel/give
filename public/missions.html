We believe generosity is a form of worship. We love to display our gratitude and devotion to God through tithes and offerings from the blessings God has placed in our hands.

Missions Giving and Pledges

Missions giving and pledges helps support long-term missionaries. We support missionaries who have been sent into key locations throughout the world. Offerings given to this fund help Bethel leadership support our financial commitments to our sons and daughters in the field.

Tracy Evans Ministry

Tracy Evans and her team are dedicated to reversing the cycles of poverty, disease and death in Mozambique, Africa. We do this in word and in deed, as we proclaim the Gospel of Jesus Christ to those we live with and minister to. Through our clinic and nutrition program, we treat 600 orphan infants, HIV+ children plus their care-givers. We have a hospice/recovery center, a pre-school and kindergarten, two prison ministries, 22 church plants, leadership classes for government officials, and pastors, and a farming program.

Mozambique Baby Clinic - Tracy Evans

Mozambique Milk Program provides milk for over 600 African orphan infants, HIV positive babies and handicapped children on a daily basis. Cameron and Jen Gamble

Cameron and Jen Gamble are the founders of Project TAKEN, a unique organization focused on training missionaries and ministries to prevent, survive, and escape hostile situation. They empower missionaries by radically redefining courage and wisdom while in the midst of uncertainty. Cameron has been training the U.S. Military Special Ops community since shortly after September 11, 2001 on survival tactics. Their heart is to impart the same elite training, but with a supernatural approach for those who dare to pierce the darkness with the light of the Gospel. They work closely with Bethel missions, BSSM short-term missions, high-risk ministries involved with human trafficking and churches globally. Additionally, they offer in-country resolution for missionaries and ministries that are facing serious threats by traveling to the source of the problem in order to aid.
